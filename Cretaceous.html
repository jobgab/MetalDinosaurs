Welcome to the METAL Cretaceous

If the Triassic was the chaotic beginning of the dinosaur saga, the Cretaceous was its loud, dramatic final act—like the explosive closing track of a metal album.

By this time dinosaurs ruled nearly every ecosystem on Earth, from colossal hunters like Tyrannosaurus rex to strange horned giants like Triceratops, while oceans were patrolled by massive marine reptiles and the skies filled with flying predators.

Flowering plants were spreading across the planet, reshaping landscapes and feeding vast herds of herbivores, and the world was warmer and teeming with life.

But just as the dinosaur empire seemed unstoppable, everything changed in a single catastrophic moment that ended the reign of the non-avian dinosaurs—closing the most legendary chapter in Earth’s prehistoric history.
