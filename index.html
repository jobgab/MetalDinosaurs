Welcome to the Metalzoic

Dinosaurs ruled Earth for millions of years. And if you ask me I would say they were pretty badass rulers! Massive predators, armored tanks, and creatures with claws, horns, and spikes made this one of the most brutal and awesome eras in Earth’s history. Compared to them, I feel like us humans a bit of a downgrade, not going to lie.

So today we are going to dive into the age when dinosaurs (and other cool creatures) roamed Earth, and discuss why even while extinct, the Mesozoic Era was more metal than us

Dive into the Triassic

Picture the early days of the Triassic like the opening chapter of a ridiculously intense metal saga: Earth had just crawled out of the ashes of the catastrophic Permian–Triassic extinction event, where most life on the planet vanished, leaving behind a harsh, almost empty world.

Across the vast supercontinent Pangaea, scorching deserts stretched for thousands of miles while strange reptilian survivors battled for dominance. Dinosaurs weren’t the kings yet—they were small newcomers sneaking around while terrifying crocodile-like predators from the Archosaur family stomped across the land like armored nightmares.

Meanwhile the planet itself rumbled with massive VOLCANIC ACTIVITY!! Pumping lava and gases into the sky and reshaping the climate. In this brutal, chaotic world of fire, deserts, and reptilian monsters, the first dinosaurs quietly appeared, small, fast survivors waiting for their moment, setting the stage for the legendary age that would follow...
